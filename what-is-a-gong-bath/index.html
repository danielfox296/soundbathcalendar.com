Glossary

What is a gong bath?

A gong bath is a sound bath where one or more large gongs are the main instrument. You lie down, stay dressed, and listen for roughly 45 to 75 minutes while the player builds waves of sound that are felt in the body as much as heard. No beliefs and no experience are required.

See upcoming gong baths

What makes a gong different

A large gong does not produce one clean note. Struck softly with a padded mallet, it releases a dense wash of overtones that swell, beat against each other, and keep evolving long after the strike. Players work in slow crescendos: the sound starts as a shimmer, builds into something you feel in your chest and the floor, then falls away again. That physical, whole-room quality is what people mean when they call a gong bath immersive.

Singing bowls, by contrast, hold steadier and more focused tones. Many sessions on the calendar use both; a session billed specifically as a gong bath is telling you the gong leads.

What a session looks like

The format is the same as any sound bath: you arrive, set up on a mat with a blanket, and lie still while the facilitator plays. There is nothing to do and no technique to get right. If you have never been to any kind of sound session, the first-timer's guide walks through the whole thing, from what to wear to whether you can fall asleep.

Is a gong bath loud?

It can be, at its peaks. A gong bath has a wider dynamic range than a bowl-led session: long quiet stretches, and crescendos that get genuinely big. Most players keep the peaks short and controlled, but if you are sound-sensitive, sit farther from the gong, bring earplugs (you will still feel the sound), or ask the facilitator beforehand how intense the session gets. They are used to the question.

Gong bath vs. sound bath — what is the difference?

A gong bath is one kind of sound bath. "Sound bath" is the umbrella term for any lie-down-and-listen session, whatever the instruments; a gong bath centers gongs specifically. If a listing just says "sound bath," expect a mix — usually bowls, chimes, and often a gong among them.

How long does a gong bath last?

Typically 45 to 75 minutes, like most sound baths. Each listing on the calendar shows its own start time, length where the operator provides it, and price.

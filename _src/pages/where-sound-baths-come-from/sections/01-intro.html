Where sound baths come from

The sound bath as you will find it on this calendar — a room of mats, an hour of gongs and bowls — is a recent, largely Western wellness format, taking shape over the last century and booming in the last fifteen years. But it is built from much older sources: gong traditions of East and Southeast Asia, India's contemplative practice of nada yoga, and the metal bowls of the Himalayan region, whose story as marketed today is more complicated than the "ancient Tibetan ritual" label suggests. The traditions deserve honest credit, and the honest version is more interesting than the marketing.

432 Hz, explained honestly

432 Hz is an alternative tuning standard: the note A tuned to 432 vibrations per second instead of the modern convention of 440. Many musicians and facilitators love how it feels, tune their instruments to it with real care, and hear a difference that matters to them. Research so far has not found effects unique to the tuning itself — so the honest way to hold 432 is as a matter of taste and craft, with the bigger claims still an open question the evidence has not answered in its favor.

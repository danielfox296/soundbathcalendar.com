Sound bath vs. meditation

The short version: meditation is usually an active practice — you work with your attention, on purpose, using a technique. A sound bath is receptive — you lie down and the sound comes to you, no technique required. They point at similar states of rest, but they ask very different things of you, which is why sound baths often work for people who say they cannot meditate.

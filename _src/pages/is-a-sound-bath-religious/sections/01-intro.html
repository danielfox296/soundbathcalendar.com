Is a sound bath religious?

No. A sound bath is a secular relaxation practice by default: you lie down and listen to instruments for about an hour, and nothing is asked of your beliefs. Some sessions do add ceremonial elements — chanting, a cacao drink, spiritual framing — and when they do, the listing says so. This page is for two people at once: the person of faith wondering whether attending conflicts with their practice, and the skeptic who does not want to be preached at. Both hesitations are reasonable, and both have honest answers.

Common questions

What is a gong bath?

A gong bath is a sound bath centered on the gong. You lie down, usually on a mat, while a facilitator plays one or more gongs; the sustained tone is louder and more physical than a bowls-only session. You stay clothed and still, and most run 45 to 75 minutes.

Is a gong bath too loud or intense for a first-timer?

Gongs can get loud at peaks, but facilitators build up and back down rather than starting there. If you are sound-sensitive, sit toward the edge of the room and tell the facilitator beforehand; you can cover your ears or step out at any point.

How much do these sessions cost?

The sessions listed here run $25 to $60. Some are offered by donation or free. Each listing shows its own price, and the ticket link goes straight to the operator.
